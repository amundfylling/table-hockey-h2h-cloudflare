# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Competitive table hockey players and fans who want to compare two players head to head. They are likely checking past results before or after tournaments, settling matchup questions, reviewing rivalries, or looking for patterns in player performance across stages and years.

## Product Purpose

Table Hockey H2H helps users quickly answer how two table hockey players have performed against each other. It should make player lookup, matchup comparison, filtering, and match inspection fast and trustworthy, while keeping the interface readable on mobile.

Success means a user can pick two players, understand the overall record, filter to the matches that matter, and inspect the underlying game list without feeling like they are working in a spreadsheet.

Future comparison work should support playoff series context as well as individual games. Playoffs are often best of 3, 5, or 7, so the product should eventually distinguish single-game results from series-level outcomes and make both useful without confusing the core head-to-head view.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, credible, sporting.

The interface should feel like a serious stats tool for a niche competitive sport: focused, precise, and confident without becoming dry or generic.

## Anti-references

This should not feel like a spreadsheet, a generic SaaS dashboard, a sportsbook, or an overly polished startup landing page.

Avoid visual patterns that make the data feel templated or corporate: identical card grids, decorative dashboard chrome, vague metric tiles, and styling that gets in the way of comparison.

## Design Principles

1. Comparison first: every screen should make the two-player matchup easier to understand.
2. Evidence stays close: summary stats, charts, filters, and the match list should feel connected rather than like separate widgets.
3. Fast scanning beats decoration: hierarchy, spacing, and contrast should help users find the winning record, stage split, dates, tournaments, and outlier matches quickly.
4. Serious but not sterile: the app should carry the energy of competitive sport while preserving analytical clarity.
5. Mobile is a primary workflow: player selection, filters, charts, and match rows must remain usable on small screens with comfortable tap targets and no horizontal guesswork.
6. Series-aware over time: future playoff features should make best-of series legible without burying individual game evidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Prioritize strong contrast, readable type, clear focus states, keyboard-friendly controls, and mobile-friendly tap targets. The player color system should not rely on color alone; labels, position, and text must also distinguish the two players. Motion should be restrained and should not interfere with scanning or mobile use.
